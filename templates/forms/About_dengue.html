Dengue: A Comprehensive Overview

Overview

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection that causes flu-like symptoms and can develop into severe complications such as dengue hemorrhagic fever and dengue shock syndrome.

Symptoms

  • High fever
  • Severe headaches
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Fatigue and nausea
  • Skin rashes
  • Bleeding (e.g., gums, nose)

Causes & Transmission

Dengue is caused by the dengue virus (DENV), which is transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, primarily Aedes aegypti. The virus spreads through mosquito bites, with infection risk highest in tropical and subtropical regions.

Diagnosis

Dengue is diagnosed through blood tests that detect the virus, viral antigens, or antibodies. Common tests include the NS1 antigen test, PCR, and IgM/IgG antibody tests.

Prevention

  • Eliminating mosquito breeding sites
  • Using insect repellents
  • Wearing protective clothing
  • Installing mosquito nets
  • Community-wide vector control measures
  • Vaccination (where available)

Treatment

There is no specific antiviral treatment for dengue. Management focuses on symptom relief, hydration, and monitoring for severe complications requiring hospitalization.

Global Impact

Dengue is a major public health concern, affecting millions of people annually. Outbreaks occur frequently in Asia, South America, and Africa, leading to increased disease burden and economic impact.

Recent Research

Ongoing research aims to develop better vaccines, antiviral treatments, and mosquito control strategies to reduce dengue transmission and severity.
